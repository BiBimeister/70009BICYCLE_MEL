Explore Cycling in Melbourne
While an increasing number of people are becoming health conscious, bicycles have turned out to be a popular way for citizens in Metropolitan Melbourne to exercise and commute.

This project is designed to present the increasing demands of bicycle related information for two different user groups: the local citizens and the state government. For the local citizens, the website can help the users find the nearest bicycle stops, bicycle shops and bicycle docks by listing all this information in an interactive map. For the state government, the website can provide bicycle usage volume and bicycle crashes analysis, which can help the state government improve the bicycle administration system.

Moreover, the website can provide the weather report and additional analysis so that the state government can make forecasts and advice on whether the day is suitable for travelling by bicycle.

The Stats!
-The ratio of alcohol related accidents-

The chart shows the ratio relation between bicycle and motor vehicle accidents within all the last 5 years alcohol related accidents.

From the chart we can see that the ratio of alcohol related accidents in bicycle accidents is 0.4%, which is much lower than motor vehicle accidents' 3.7%.

The chart indicates that it is less possible that the bicycle accidents are related to alcohol. The government can pay less attention to drunk bicycle driving.

-Vehicle accidents per day of the week-

This chart shows the accident numbers over the last 5 years in different days of the week, it also shows the difference between bicycle accidents and motor vehicle accidents.

From the chart we can see that the pattern differs in bicycle accidents and motor vehicle accidents. The peak of bicycle accidents occurs on Wendesday while the peak of motor vehicle accidents occurs on Friday.

The chart indicates that on Wednesday, bicycle accidents are more often than other days of week. The authorities can pay more attention to Wednesday to ensure traffic safety related to bicycle use.

-Speed zones of bicycle accidents in Melbourne-

This chart shows the ratio of different speed zones where bicycle accidents happen.

From the chart we can see that bicycle accidents mainly happen between 40-60 km/h. Moreover, higher the speed zone, greater the possibility of a bicycle accident.

The chart indicates that the majority of the bicycle accidents happen at 40-60km/h. Considering the 60km/h speed zone has the most bicycle accidents and the most fatalities, the government should put more warning signs at 60km/h speed zone areas.
